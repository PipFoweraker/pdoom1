## Working rules

1. Rivals never play human instruments; humans never play clean synthesis.
   The timbre boundary IS the story boundary.
2. Doom has no theme. Hearing OUR material go wrong is scarier than a villain
   theme, and it implements "doom is a layer, not a repaint" motivically.
3. The rival's harmonic indifference is the point: it climbs in its own grid
   over whatever we are playing. Do not harmonize it. Dissonance against it is
   correct and free.
4. The green cell is the game's E1M1: it may appear in any tier warped by the
   doom ladder, and once, pure, at victory.
5. Anathem/mathic register reserved for structural story moments (intro,
   time-loop, denouement) so it stays special. Convergence noted: Anathem's
   avout are monastics whose liturgy IS mathematics -- the math-chant conceit
   is native to that register, not borrowed decoration.

## Trailer-specific purposes (start/end pieces vs loops)

Loops conceal seams and sustain; trailers are RHETORIC: introduce motifs,
tease mechanics, ship the feel, then END (crescendo, cut, button). The v0.2
ending grammar: overwhelm made inevitable but never arriving; hard cut;
silence; one footstep. Candidate mechanics-tease mappings for a future pass:
- turn IMPACT / "Next!" = the trudge downbeat (already doing this)
- doom meter rising = rival recurrence-gap halving (already doing this)
- action selection / plan phase = green-cell fragments surfacing mid-trudge
- the doom-band axes = one WEIRD smear moment (blue flicker) before act 3
