1a. Taiko -- "The Skip" (the Drifting Houses seven)

For: taiko player(s). Source: patches/drifting_seven_v0_1.js (decoded from the isolated drum stem, 2026-07-18).
DON (su) don ka doko DON (su) DON doko doko DON-ka
One bar of 7/4 grouped 3+2+2 at 129. Legend: bottom-space F = o-daiko (the deep pickup), middle B = chu-daiko main strokes, C above = lighter strokes (gallop), X-ish top E = metal tick (atarigane doubles this). The two accented offbeats mid-bar are the LOUDEST hits in the cell -- that offbeat pair IS the skip. Beat 7 leans hard into the next downbeat (the pickup is the loop announcement).
Record: 2+ minutes steady solo with click, then one "human drift" take with no click. Room mics welcome -- we want the air. Also record each drum's single strokes, all dynamics, full decay.

1b. Taiko -- "The Trudge" (footsteps in the snow)

  
For: taiko player(s). Source: patches/trudge_welcome_v0_1.js
  (the defeat cue -- "I love the dirge").

  

  
DON . . do . . don .   (limping 3+3+2 -- heavy 1, ghost 4 and 7)

  

    Slow 4/4 at 63, steps on the 3+3+2 eighth grid. Beat 1 heavy;
    the two later steps are GHOSTED (about 60-75% weight). Every so often --
    deliberately, maybe once in twelve bars -- drop a step entirely.
    The walker is tired. Soft mallets or palm strokes; round edges, no crack.
  

  
Record: 2+ minutes steady, plus a no-click take, plus a
  take with more dropped steps than feels comfortable. Also: long single
  strikes with full decay (zen standoff punctuation), and 2 minutes of
  quiet continuous "rain" roll (tier-2/3 texture).

2. Atarigane / chan-chiki -- the timekeeper

  
For: the metal-drum timekeeper. Doubles as the terminal
  tier's "alarm that will not hurry".

  

  

    Three asks: (1) steady eighth tick, 2 minutes each at 63 and
    104; (2) the skip cell's timeline (top line above -- the two
    ticks sit where the taiko's ka strokes fall); (3) a sparse unhurried
    "alarm" pair at 96: two dry ticks per bar, never rushing, never louder.
  

  
Record: dry and close. The alarm take should sound like
  it does not care whether you are running out of time. Because you are.

3b. Shakuhachi -- zen standoff phrases (D minor pentatonic)

  
For: shakuhachi player. Source: patches/zen_standoff_sketch_v0_1.js.

  

  

    Breath-paced, ~60. The written rests are REAL -- a full silent
    bar between phrases, longer if it feels right. Endings hang unresolved;
    do not land home. Occasionally answer a phrase an octave up, half
    volume (the mountain echoing you). Rubato throughout -- the click is
    only for the drone.
  

  
Record: each phrase several times with generous silence;
  a few long single tones with natural vibrato bloom, every dynamic from
  barely-there to full. Silence is a note -- leave it in the takes.

4. Upright bass -- plod, roots, pedals

  
For: the standing-bass favour-ower.

  

  

    Line 1, the trudge plod at 63: D-A-D on the limping 3+3+2 grid,
    dragging a hair BEHIND the beat (the drums lead, you lean back). Written
    an octave up for reading; sound it as low as the instrument allows --
    low D preferred if you have the extension, else the written octave is
    fine. Both arco and pizz takes. Line 2, the green-bed walk at
    104: roots on the I-vi-IV-V lane in C, two-feel. Line 3: long
    pedal tones on D and C, arco, straight and even -- these become
    drone beds.
  

  
Record: 2+ minutes per line. For pedals: bow changes as
  smooth as you can, we will loop around them.

5. Pip -- the math chant kit

  
For: Pip (RODE USB mic, quietest room, dry). Reciting
  tone: D (hum the trudge drone first, then speak-chant on it).

  

  

    Delivery per phrase: repeat, repeat, repeat, tail-variant (the
    AAAB shape again -- same law as the dirge). Three registers to test on
    mic day, all on the D reciting tone:


    1. Greek-letter litany (the update rule as pure letters):

    the-ta ... e-ta ... na-bla the-ta ... the-ta

    2. Latin machine-liturgy (40k register, learn-from-not-copy):

    gra-di-ens de-scen-dit ... er-ror mi-nor fit

    3. Pure math vocables (MMoB texture, zero semantics):

    del ... sig ... eps mu del ... sig


    Stack imperfect takes -- the misalignments ARE the choir. Also record:
    low sustained drones on D, whatever the voice gives honestly.
  

  
Hard line (Pip's ruling, standing): real sutras/mantras
  and personal practice material stay OUT. The math is the sacred register

6. Modular synth friends -- the rival voice

  
For: modular/DJ friends. The ONE legitimately synthetic
  voice in the score (casting rule: rivals never play human instruments).

  

  

    In D, bone-dry, gridded, no room, no dynamics, never misses.
    Asks: (1) the rising three-note call (written above -- it transposes UP
    on each recurrence and does NOT care what key the humans are in);
    (2) gridded blips, 16th runs; (3) one "impossible speed" texture --
    a pattern accelerated past human playability into a wall.
  

  
Hands-on modular patching beats laptop presets here --
