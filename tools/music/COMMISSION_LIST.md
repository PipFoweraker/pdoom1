# Commission list -- what we need from real players (draft 1, 2026-07-18)

Per CASTING_SHEET.md. Every item below has a Strudel reference patch the
player can hear first. Proper engraved notation (staff/percussion notation,
lilypond or clean PDFs) is the NEXT session's deliverable -- this list is the
who-and-what so Pip can start conversations.

## 1. Taiko player(s) [Pip knows locally]

- The SKIP cell: 7-beat bar grouped 3+2+2 at ~129 bpm. Accents on 1 and 2,
  loudest on the &-of-3 and &-of-4, gallop through 5-6-7, beat 7 leans hard
  into the next downbeat. Reference: patches/drifting_seven_v0_1.js and the
  isolated drum stem of the source track.
- The TRUDGE step: limping 3+3+2 in slow 4/4 at 63 bpm, heavy 1, ghost 4 and 7.
  Occasional deliberately dropped step. Reference: trudge_welcome_v0_1.js.
- Long single strikes with full decay (zen standoff punctuation) + quiet
  continuous "rain" rolls (M2/M3 texture).
- Record: each pattern solo, steady, 2+ minutes, plus one "human drift" take
  (no click). Room mics welcome -- we want the air.

## 2. Atarigane / chan-chiki (timekeeper metal)

- Steady 8th tick at 63 and 104 bpm (2 min each), plus the skip cell's
  timeline. Doubles as the M4 "alarm that will not hurry".

## 3. Shakuhachi player [Pip knows locally]

- The dirge, ornamented freely: D dorian, phrases "re fa mi" / "re fa la
  (mi-fa) re" (engraving to follow; audio ref: dirge_variations_v0_1.js V7).
- Zen standoff phrases: D minor pentatonic, breath-paced, LONG silences
  between phrases; unresolved endings. Reference: zen_standoff_sketch_v0_1.js.
- A few long single tones with natural vibrato bloom, all dynamics.

## 4. Upright / double bass [the favour-ower]

- Trudge plod: D1-A1-D1 pattern at 63 bpm, arco AND pizz takes.
- Green-bed walking roots at 104 bpm in C (I-vi-IV-V lane).
- Long low D and C pedal tones, arco, for drone beds.

## 5. Pip (voice) -- see chant kit in DIRECTION_NOTES

- Math chant experiments: Greek-letter litany / Latin machine-liturgy /
  math-phoneme vocables. RODE USB mic, dry, quiet room, stacked takes.
  Delivery: repeat-repeat-repeat then tail variant, on a D reciting tone
  (chant over trudge_welcome_v0_1.js drone).
- Low sustained drones (Gyuto-adjacent, whatever the voice gives honestly).
- Voiceover/narration is a separate later session, same setup.

## 6. Modular synth + DJ friends

- The RIVAL voice is deliberately synthetic -- but hands-on modular beats
  laptop presets (physical rule). Ask for: clean rising 3-note calls, gridded
  blips, one "impossible speed" texture, all bone-dry, in D.
- DJ ears: judgment passes on transitions/crossfades once stems exist, and
  the terminal-phase "wall" texture.

## Recording specs (all commissions)

44.1 kHz WAV, dry (no reverb/FX), tuning A=440, click provided where noted.
Per drop-in kit: stems for the game must eventually loop seamlessly at
~-16 LUFS; performers just deliver clean takes -- looping/mastering is on us.
Rights: simple written note that recordings are contributed to the project
(same as the friend's DJ-set agreement flagged in STEM_CATALOGUE.md).
