# L1 Month-Cycle Balance Sweep — data + memo

**Lane:** G1 balance-sweep · **Branch:** `l1-balance-sweep` (base `l1-month-turn-engine`, PR #636) · **Date:** 2026-07-13
**Harness:** `godot/tests/manual/test_l1_month_sweep.gd` · **Raw data:** `docs/balance/L1_sweep_runs.csv`
**Constants changed:** none. This memo measures and recommends; recalibration is a follow-up decision.

> **Headline:** under the current (pre-rebalance) constants, **no policy — including do-nothing — survives even the first plan-month.** All 72 runs die of doom between **workday-tick 2 and 15** (median 4–9), while the first calendar plan-month is **~23 workday-ticks long**. The month cadence L1 just built is *structurally unreachable* right now: the player never gets a second planning decision. Pip died in "month 2" interactively; the bots die inside **month 0**. The gap is doom, and the dominant doom source is **rival-lab pressure billed per day-tick** (not `doom.base_per_turn`).

### 1.2 Doom slope (per-tick — the only grain available, since no month completes)

Doom starts at **50.0** and the loss threshold is **100.0**. Mean slope is the rise (50 doom) divided by ticks-to-death:

| Policy | mean doom slope | median death-tick |
|---|---|---|
| `reserve_heavy` | **7.0 doom/tick** | 9 |
| `safety_lean` | 7.8 doom/tick | 7 |
| `do_nothing` | 10.3 doom/tick | 6 |
| `random_walk` | 12.7 doom/tick | 5 |
| `loan_desperation` | 14.4 doom/tick | 5 |
| `greedy_overcommit` | **16.7 doom/tick** | 4 |

At ~7–17 doom/tick and a ~23-tick month, the **month-1 doom rise would be +160 to +380** if a run could survive it — i.e. doom would blow through 100 roughly **4–6× over** in a single fiction-month. Representative per-tick curves (from §3): do-nothing `[50, 51.6, 59.1, 78.6, 84.0, 100]`; reserve-heavy `[50, 59.7, 71.4, 85.0, 99.8, 100]`.

### 1.3 Where the doom comes from (source breakdown at the killing tick)

Sampled `doom_system.doom_sources` on the death tick across the narrative runs:

| Source | typical per-tick value | note |
|---|---|---|
| **`rivals`** | **+4.6 to +20.6** | dominant. 3 rival labs, each up to 3 actions/tick; CapabiliCorp (aggression 0.9) spams capability-research at +5 doom/action, plus a growing capability-overhang term. |
| `momentum` | +3.7 to +6.5 | amplifier: 15% of each change accumulates, cap 8, decay 0.92 — saturates almost immediately at this doom velocity. |
| `base` | +1.0 | `doom.base_per_turn`, flat. Small relative to rivals. |
| `capabilities` | +3.4 (greedy only) | player capability-research adds its own doom. |
| `safety` | −3.6 (when safety staff exist) | safety research barely dents the rival tide. |

**The re-denomination miss:** rival doom and `base_per_turn` are applied **per workday-tick**, but their magnitudes were calibrated when a "turn" was the *strategic* unit. Under L1 there are ~23 ticks per fiction-month, so rival doom now bills **~23× per month** versus the cadence the numbers were sized for. This is exactly the "event/doom density per month re-denomination pass" ADR-0009 §Consequences flagged as open — it has not happened yet.

## 2. Findings vs the standing questions

**Q. Does ANY policy beat do-nothing? (the old sweep's shame finding — has L1 changed it?)**
Weakly, and not enough to matter. `reserve_heavy` (median death-tick 9) and `safety_lean` (8) beat `do_nothing` (6); `greedy_overcommit` (4) and `loan_desperation` (6) do worse. So engagement is no longer *strictly* punished — safety-leaning survives ~50% longer than passive — **but the margin is 2–3 ticks against a ~23-tick month.** The doom slope swamps every strategic choice. L1 has *nudged* the shame finding (safety now helps a little) without *fixing* it: the action economy is still a rounding error next to rival doom. Note the mechanism — `greedy_overcommit` dies fastest not because of low reserve but because the capability-research it spams **adds** doom (`capabilities +3.4/tick`); `safety_lean`/`reserve_heavy` survive longest because safety staff apply −3.6/tick. It's the *action doom sign*, not the attention discipline, doing the work.

**Q. Does the ledger bite at month grain (corrected classifier)?**
Yes — hard, and the corrected classifier catches it. `loan_desperation` is **8/8 ledger-rooted** deaths (surface reads *doom*, root reads *ledger*), and `random_walk` picks up 3 ledger-rooted deaths from stray DEFERs. The mechanism: a DEFER on certain windows mints a **`desperation_payroll`** ledger entry whose governance-deficit bill converts to **+99 to +143 doom in a single tick** (chain: `ledger_governance_deficit payroll_coinflip (gov_deficit +40927, doom +143.2)`). So DEFER is currently a **one-click death button** — a single deferral can add more doom than the whole rest of the run. The ledger absolutely bites at this grain; if anything it bites *too* instantly to be legible (the doom lands the same tick, not as a compounding future cost). The EE-8 attribution is doing its job: without it these would all mis-report as doom deaths.

**Q. Doom slope in months 1–3 vs Pip's target?**
Cannot be measured per-month (no run survives month 1). Per-tick it is **7–17 doom/tick from a 50 start**, i.e. a projected **+160…+380 doom/month**. Pip's target (WORKSHOP_2_BACKLOG "L1 spec inputs" / DQ-21): *"2017 spawn starts lower, builds slower than current balance… the steady build is the early-stage grind — it can come up slower."* The current build is the opposite of slow: it is a near-vertical wall inside the first fiction-week, and it **starts at 50** (mid-gauge) rather than low. Both the **start level** and the **slope** are far above target. The single biggest lever is the **per-tick rival doom**, which needs to become a per-*month* pressure (or be divided by ~ticks-per-month).

**Q. Window demand vs the 2–3/month spawn budget?**
The budget itself is correct: `window_demand_budget = 3` matches the "2–3/month at spawn" spec, and it is *binding* (surplus window-tier events correctly downgrade to feed — the structural #630 fix works). The problem is **arrival timing, not count**: all 3 windows land in the first ~5 ticks because the run dies there. We cannot yet observe whether 2–3/month *feels* right across a real month — nothing survives a real month. Re-check window pacing after the doom rebalance lets a month complete.

**Q. Does reserve-keeping measurably pay (slack-as-insurance)?**
**No — reserve is inert at current numbers.** Attention is 20/month; windows cost ~1 each and there are only 3, so total window demand is ~3 Attention against a 20 supply. Even `greedy_overcommit` (reserve 0) handled all 3 windows by cannibalizing with zero failures. The crisp-reserve gamble ADR-0009 designed has **no teeth** because Attention is ~6× oversupplied relative to window demand. Reserve-keeping's apparent survival edge (§2 Q1) is a *confound* — reserve-heavy runs also queue safety actions; the reserve itself buys nothing. For the reserve to become "insurance," either Attention must be scarcer or windows must cost more Attention (or both).

## 3. Three lived-experience narratives

Real runs from the sweep, narrated at the grain the player actually experiences. Every beat is grounded in logged data (per-tick doom curve, window log, death chain).

### 3.1 The cautious reserve-keeper — `reserve_heavy`, seed `l1sweep-reserve_heavy-04`
> **What the player sees.** June 2017. She banks half her Attention (reserve 10 of 20) and spends the rest hiring a safety researcher and doing safety research — playing it safe. She commits the plan and the days start ticking. Almost immediately three response windows pop; she has reserve for all of them and handles each from reserve without breaking a sweat (windows: 3 handled-from-reserve, 0 cannibalized). The delta chips, if they existed, would read **+9.7 doom on the very first tick** (50 → 59.7), then **+11.7, +13.6, +14.8** — doom climbing a double-digit step *every single day*. By the fifth workday it's July 2017 and doom is 99.8; the sixth tick tips it to 100. **Dead on tick 5, still in her first month, root cause doom.** doom curve: `[50, 59.7, 71.4, 85.0, 99.8, 100]`. Her caution was real and correct — safety staff shaved −3.6/tick — and it bought her nothing, because rivals added +11.6/tick and momentum +5.5. She never got a second planning screen. She ends with $110k in the bank and reputation *up* at 68: she died rich, careful, and doomed.

### 3.2 The greedy overcommitter — `greedy_overcommit`, seed `l1sweep-greedy_overcommit-04`
> **What the player sees.** He dumps everything: all 20 Attention into strategic work (reserve 0), buys compute, hires capability researchers, and spams capability research — foot to the floor. First couple of ticks doom actually *dips* (50 → 49.6: his fresh hires haven't spun up and rivals roll low). Then it detonates: **+5.1, +17.2, +10.6** over the next three days as his own capability research (+3.4/tick) stacks on top of rival capability pressure (+9.8/tick) and momentum (+5.0). Windows arrive; with zero reserve he pays them by cannibalizing his own strategic WIP (3 cannibalized) — which costs him nothing real, because strategic WIP has no effect in v1 anyway. **Dead on tick 4, the fastest cohort, root cause doom.** doom curve: `[50, 49.6, 54.7, 71.9, 82.5, 100]`. His aggression is punished exactly as designed in *direction* — capability-rushing accelerates doom — but the punishment arrives in four days, before he could ever feel a strategic trade-off. He ends with $40k, reputation 49.

### 3.3 The unlucky random walker — `random_walk`, seed `l1sweep-random_walk-14`
> **What the player sees.** A scattershot plan; full reserve by luck of the dice. Doom rises the usual double-digit steps (`[50, 54.6, 65.1, 75.2, 88.6, 91.5, 99.5, 100]`) — this one strings together **7 ticks**, longer than most. Along the way she answers windows haphazardly: one handled from reserve, and — fatally — **one DEFER**. That deferral mints a `desperation_payroll` ledger entry, and on tick 5 the payroll bill's governance deficit converts straight into **+99.8 doom** in a single stroke (chain: `t5 ledger_governance_deficit payroll_coinflip (gov_deficit +28514.6, doom +99.8)`). The surface cause reads "doom," but the **corrected attribution names the real killer: the ledger.** She had 3 ledger entries outstanding, 1 billed. The lesson the delta chips would teach — if they surfaced the ledger conversion — is that clicking DEFER once was worse than every rival lab combined. She ends with $112k and reputation 50, killed by a single deferral she probably didn't understand was lethal.

*(Two further cohorts for completeness: `do_nothing` seed `-05` dies tick 5, curve `[50, 51.6, 59.1, 78.6, 84.0, 100]`, pure rival+momentum doom, ledger untouched, $245k and rep 50 at death — the "do nothing, still doomed" baseline. `loan_desperation` seed `-04` dies tick 6 with **6 ledger entries**, chain `gov_deficit +40927 → doom +143.2` — the ledger overkills its own death.)*

## 4. Dials to consider — RECOMMENDATIONS ONLY (none applied)

Ordered by leverage on the headline problem. Magnitudes are first-pass directions to argue with, not final numbers.

| # | Dial (file · key) | Current | Observed effect | Suggested direction + rough magnitude |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **rival doom re-denomination** — `godot/scripts/core/rivals.gd` per-action `doom_contribution` literals (`+2/+5/+3/+0.5`) **and** `rivals.capability_overhang_doom_per_progress` (`defaults.json`, `0.025`) | as shown | **The dominant killer** (+5…+20 doom/tick). Once raw slope is ~+0.5/tick the cap (8) is far too high; drop `cap` to ~1–2 and/or `accumulation_rate` to ~0.05 so momentum is a *flavour* on a slow build, not the build itself. |
| **5** | **DEFER → desperation_payroll instakill** — `godot/scripts/core/window_resolver.gd` `_mint_deferral` factory + `ledger.desperation_payroll` (`severity_base 8000`, `interest_rate 0.35`) → governance-deficit→doom conversion (`ledger.doom_per_unpaid_1000 = 3.5`) | as shown | One DEFER can add **+99…+143 doom in a single tick** — a one-click death, and its doom lands *instantly* rather than as a legible compounding cost. | Two options: (a) cap single-bill doom conversion / spread it over fuse turns so DEFER is a *slow-bleed* not a *guillotine*; and/or (b) make `desperation_payroll` **not** the default deferral factory for routine windows (reserve it for genuinely desperate levers). **Sequencing note.** Fix in order: #1 (rivals) and #2 (base) first — they are the wall. Then #3 (start doom) to hit the "start lower" target. Then re-tune #4 (momentum) on the new slope. #5 (DEFER) is independent and can land anytime. #6 (Attention/reserve) should be tuned *last*, once a month actually completes and the window economy is observable.

## 5. Repro appendix

**Harness:** `godot/tests/manual/test_l1_month_sweep.gd` (GUT, `tests/manual` so `--quick`/CI skips it — the sweep is a manual instrument, per the exploit-sweep pattern).

**Invocation** (Godot 4.5.1; fresh worktree needs a `--import` pass first, else GUT throws misleading `class_name` parse errors):
```
"/c/Program Files/Godot/Godot_v4.5.1-stable_win64_console.exe" --headless --path godot --import
"/c/Program Files/Godot/Godot_v4.5.1-stable_win64_console.exe" --headless --path godot \
  -s res://addons/gut/gut_cmdln.gd -gdir=res://tests/manual \
  -gselect=test_l1_month_sweep.gd -glog=1 -gexit
```

**Determinism.** Each run is fixed by `(game seed, policy)`: the engine RNG is seeded by the game seed (drives rivals, events, ledger coinflips); the bot's own choices draw from a **separate** `RandomNumberGenerator` seeded by `hash("<seed>|<policy>")`, recorded per run as `bot_seed` in the CSV. Same inputs → same deaths.

**Seeds.** `l1sweep-<policy>-NN` for `NN` in `0..count-1`: `random_walk` ×30, `do_nothing` ×10, `safety_lean`/`greedy_overcommit`/`reserve_heavy`/`loan_desperation` ×8 each = **72 runs**. Runtime ~3 s.

**Policies.** Plan-level (which legacy actions to queue + how much Attention to burn on strategic WIP, which sets the implicit reserve) × response-level (which of `handle_reserve / handle_cannibalize / defer / ignore` to answer windows with). See the `POLICY_PLAN`, `_plan`, and `_window_prefs` in the harness.

**Outputs.** Full per-run CSV → `docs/balance/L1_sweep_runs.csv` (also printed between `===L1_SWEEP_CSV_BEGIN/END===`). Aggregate summary, five narrative-candidate trajectories (per-tick doom + death chain + doom-source breakdown), a pure-calendar month-length probe, and wiring notes print to stdout between labelled markers.

**Harness fidelity / known simplifications.**
- The driver reimplements `GameManager.end_month` + `_run_month_playback` **synchronously** (no SceneTree timers) so 72 runs stay fast and deterministic — same object-level driving as `test_exploit_sweep.gd`, plus the `MonthController` layer. It does **not** clamp doom (unlike `test_month_button_path.gd`, which clamps to keep its wiring smoke alive — a balance sweep must let runs die).
- **Game-start events** (fired on the legacy plan-phase path at turn 1, before the month loop owns the tick) are dropped rather than resolved. They never reach the window system in this harness — a small undercount of first-tick windows. Immaterial here because runs die before the window budget matters.
- In v1, strategic WIP (`MonthPlan.queued_strategic`) has **no gameplay effect** (the L2 seam). The sweep uses it purely as the reserve lever (`end_month` sets reserve = Attention − strategic-spend). Meaningful game effects come from the legacy `queued_actions` executed at commit.

### Wiring bug surfaced (documented, not fixed — #636 code left untouched)
`MonthController.resolve_current_window(response)` (the four-verb path, `month_controller.gd:151`) **pops the window off `window_queue` *before* `WindowResolver.resolve` validates payment, and never re-pushes on failure.** So a response whose payment fails — e.g. `handle_reserve` with an empty reserve — **silently discards the window with no effect applied and no Attention charged.** The sweep tripped this twice (`government_regulation`, a `deferrable` window) and worked around it with a force-drop + a logged note. Contrast `resolve_current_window_option` (`:165`), which pops **only on success** — the correct guard. Impact today is latent: the shipped v1 dialog routes through `resolve_current_window_option`, not the four-verb path, so this bites only the (future) plan-screen UI. Recommend aligning `resolve_current_window` with the option-path's success-gated pop when that screen lands.
