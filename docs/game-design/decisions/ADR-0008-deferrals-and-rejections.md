# ADR-0008 — Deferrals, folds, and rejections (the negative space of workshop #1)

- **Status:** ACCEPTED — negative decisions recorded so they survive
- **Date:** 2026-07-04
- **Session:** Fable workshop #1

Deferrals are sequenced, not shelved; each carries its revisit trigger. Rejections are
final unless a future session explicitly reopens them.

## Folded (the idea lives, the system dies)

- **Geopolitics** → deflated into the **cause library** (ADR-0005): "export controls
  lapse," "national AI initiative funds rival_lab," "treaty conference convenes" are
  schedule entries fed by `pdoom-data`, zero new engine systems. *Line to hold:* the
  moment someone proposes persistent geopolitical **state** (relations meters, a world
  map), that is the isolated-system trap; it waits until the content version demonstrably
  runs out of expressive room.
- **Standalone management depth** → staff are **AP-leverage with liability riders**
  (ADR-0003). Pip's ruling, preserved: *"these are the resources by which you get things
  done, given your tiny manual amount of hours per week (action points), you're going to
  hire costly humans who come with their own problems, bother."* Canon established: **AP
  are the founder's personal hours** — the one resource that never scales. Disgruntled
  ex-staff are secret ledger entries; whistleblowers are exposure events; managers are
  insurance shortening fuses on people-liabilities. Tone register for personnel problems:
  mild exasperation ("bother"), not HR-simulator gravity. *Revisit:* only if ledger-borne
  staff texture proves insufficient in playtests.
- **Blackmail** → one exposure-event genre on secret ledger entries (ADR-0003), not a
  system.

## Deferred (real, but sequenced behind the core)

- **Inward SA** (rivals running discovery on *your* ledger as a mirrored system): defer
  until the Ledger and outward SA prove out. The cheap version (secret entries + exposure
  events) ships with ADR-0003 and covers the hooks blackmail actually needs.
- **Player-chosen challenge modifiers at run start** (PoE map mods / Balatro stakes):
  parked. Friction with lexicographic turns-scoring (you can't pay someone extra turns
  for hard mode) — would need separate boards or prestige flair per modifier set.
  *Revisit:* when the community exists and asks for it.
- **Office cosmetics / employee environment**: parked as soft flavor money-sinks,
  available whenever a gentle sink is needed. No design work owed.

## Rejected (final)

- **Player-triggered disasters** (SimCity disaster-menu style): hardness is baked into
  engine/seed settings, never a player-facing trigger. (Pip, explicitly.)
- **Victory condition / victory bonus**: there is no winning, only time bought
  (ADR-0002).
- **Influence as a global resource**: influence is the receivables column of the ledger,
  per-actor (ADR-0007).
- **Score terms valuing end-state stocks**: anti-sinks, rejected on sight (ADR-0002).

## Deliberately open (not deferred — undecided on purpose)

- **Wall-clock pacing targets** (median-death minutes; deep-run hours): held open by
  Pip pending playtests of the new systems. Constraints already fixed: the distribution
  is long-tailed ("several hundred turns" as the deep-run tail, median deaths early and
  cheap); dying at turn 25 must cost minutes, not hours; the early game must carry
  founding-location strategic weight *and* be fast to replay for experienced hands.
  *Revisit trigger:* after first playtests of Ledger + scoring rewire.
