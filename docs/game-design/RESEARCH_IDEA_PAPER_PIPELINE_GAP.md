# The research -> idea -> paper pipeline gap

Status: ANALYSIS (fast-follow scoping doc, not a build brief). Read-only investigation
by an agent on 2026-07-22 at Pip's request. No code changed.

## The question (Pip's words)

> "There's a gap from when we onboard an employee and *direct* them to research an *idea*
> that they devote time to, turning into research with enough specificity that it can be
> turned into a *paper* with that idea -- this seems to close out the
> promise-action-allocation-of-AP-kinda-represented-by-staff [loop]."

Unpacked, the game's fiction/UI promises a chain of five links:

1. onboard a researcher, then
2. **direct** them toward a **specific idea/topic**, and they
3. **devote time/attention** to it, which
4. accrues into research **with enough specificity**, which
5. **converts into a paper carrying that idea.**

Pip suspects the middle links (2, 4, 5) are promised but not delivered. This doc traces
what the code actually does, names each broken link, renders a judgment, and sketches a
plan of attack.

---

## 1. The current model, concretely (what ACTUALLY happens)

### 1a. Onboard a researcher -- BUILT (link 1)

`researcher.gd` is a rich object: `specialization` (safety / capabilities /
interpretability / alignment -- a fixed LANE, set at candidate generation, never changed by
the player), `skill_level`, `appetites` (compute / prestige / mentees / money /
mission_purity -- ADR-0011 s8), a hidden `quirk`, onboarding flags, burnout, loyalty. The
hiring pipeline (candidate pool -> offered -> employed -> departed) and onboarding checklist
(laptop / visa / mentoring) are real and gate productivity
(`researcher.gd:249-279` get_effective_productivity). This link is solid.

### 1b. A researcher's "work" each turn -- BUILT, but it is an UNDIRECTED RNG DRIP

`turn_manager.gd:119-250` `_step_researcher_productivity` is the whole of what a researcher
"does":

- Per researcher, per turn: roll `research_roll < 0.30 * productivity`
  (`turn_manager.gd:178-181`). On success, generate `randi_range(1,3)` research
  (`:182`), scaled by the global `get_research_multiplier()` (Rushed/Standard/Thorough,
  `:183`), then modified by the researcher's fixed `specialization` (capabilities x1.25 and
  adds doom; safety reduces doom) (`:187-205`).
- All of it sums into one number, `research_from_employees`, and is dumped into a single
  fungible scalar: `state.add_resources({"research": research_from_employees})`
  (`turn_manager.gd:235-236`).

So a researcher's "work" resolves to: *a per-turn coin-flip that adds 1-3 points to one
global `research` pool.* There is no topic, no idea, no target, no accumulation of
anything specific. The only "direction" the player has over research is a single
**org-wide** stance -- `research_quality_mode` (rushed / standard / thorough,
`game_state.gd:474-497`, UI `research_quality_selector.gd`) -- which multiplies speed and
feeds a risk pool. It is global, not per-researcher and not per-idea.

### 1c. Can the player DIRECT a researcher toward a specific idea? -- NO

Grep across `scripts/core` + `data/actions` finds no assign / direct / focus / steer verb
for an individual researcher. `specialization` is immutable after generation. The one
strategic-menu lever near this ("Convert capability researchers to safety focus",
`strategic.json`) flips a lane in bulk; it is not "direct person X at idea Y." The
`assignment UI` and "workstreams from a backlog" are explicitly listed as **owed / not
built** (see ADR-0011 s3-4 + Consequences, and section 2 below).

### 1d. Do "ideas" exist as first-class objects? -- NO

There is no Idea / Topic-accrual / Agenda / Workstream object anywhere in `scripts/core`.
`research` is an undifferentiated float. `Topic` exists only as an enum on a *paper*
(`paper_submissions.gd:16-22`), chosen by the player at submit time -- it is a label on the
output, never a thing that accrues or gains specificity over time.

### 1e. How is a paper produced today? -- TWO disconnected paths, neither carries an accrued idea

**Path A -- the auto-published papers COUNTER (the one the HUD advertises).**
`turn_manager.gd:480-500` `_step_publish_papers`: when the fungible `state.research`
crosses a threshold (`papers.research_per_paper`, default 100), it does
`state.papers += int(research/100)` and keeps the remainder. That is the entire mechanic.
No `PaperSubmission` object is created; no topic, no lead author, no idea -- just a counter
increment. The HUD tooltip literally sells this as the pipeline: *"Generated by staff. 100
research = 1 auto-published paper."* (`main_ui.gd:547`). The `publish_paper` **action**
(`actions.gd:339-346`) is a sibling: spend 20 research + 1 AP -> `papers += 1`,
`reputation += 2`, bump `global_alarm`. Still no idea attached.

**Path B -- the conference PaperSubmission objects (Issue #468 travel system).**
`actions.submit_paper_to_conference` (`actions.gd:724-778`) is the ONLY place a
`PaperSubmission` is created in gameplay. Here the player DOES pick a `topic` (from a menu
in the submit dialog, `main_ui.gd:2965-3075`) and a `lead_researcher`, spends
`research_amount` from the fungible pool + 1 AP, and gets a paper whose `quality` is
`calculate_paper_quality(research_invested, lead_skill, coauthors, ...)`
(`paper_submissions.gd:115-131`) and whose `title` is procedurally generated from the topic
(`:133-168`). It then runs a multi-turn review/accept/reject/present lifecycle
(`:170-243`).

The two paths are unrelated: Path B does **not** increment `state.papers`; Path A never
creates a `PaperSubmission`. And critically, in Path B the paper's `topic` is chosen at
submit time from a dropdown and is **decoupled** from which researcher generated the
research or what they "worked on" -- because the research they spend is the anonymous global
pool. The `lead_researcher`'s `specialization` need not match the paper's `topic`. The
paper carries an idea the *player labels it with at the last second*, not an idea that a
researcher accrued.

### 1f. The AP / Attention economy (the "allocation" Pip references)

Two currencies coexist mid-migration. Legacy `action_points` (`game_state.gd:700-725`) is
what research actions and paper submission actually spend today (1 AP each). The ADR-0009
`Attention` layer (`month_plan.gd`) is the intended replacement -- founder decisions/month,
with staff on a *separate* per-person `actions` currency. Crucially, `month_plan.gd`
itself flags the missing layer in its header: staff-`actions` and workstream WIP are "the
seam L2 workstreams extend" (`month_plan.gd:21`, and `spend_attention` at `:78-89` is the
primitive "duration subsystems ... use"). So the "allocation-of-AP-represented-by-staff"
that Pip names is, today, just: staff passively generate research; the player spends
*founder* AP to convert a slug of that pool into a labelled paper. Staff attention is never
*allocated* to anything.

## 2. The gap, precisely (promised-but-undelivered, link by link)

| Link | Fiction / UI implies | Mechanic delivers | Verdict |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|---------|
| 1. Onboard | Hiring pipeline, cards, lanes, appetites | Full researcher + pipeline | BUILT |
| 2. Direct toward a specific idea | Candidate lanes, "Research Quality" selector, "convert to safety focus", first-author/prestige appetites on the card | Only a GLOBAL quality stance + immutable lane. No per-researcher, per-idea direction. No assignment verb exists. | NOT DELIVERED |
| 3. They devote time to it | HUD: "Generated by staff"; ADR-0011 "staff effort assigned to workstreams" | A per-turn RNG coin-flip adding 1-3 to a global pool; no target | PARTIAL (time is spent, but on nothing in particular) |
| 4. Accrues into research with SPECIFICITY | The word "research" accumulating; topic enums exist | `research` is one undifferentiated float; nothing gains specificity | NOT DELIVERED |
| 5. Converts into a paper carrying THAT idea | Submit dialog with topic + lead; procedural titles; "100 research = 1 paper" | Path A: counter++ carrying nothing. Path B: topic is a last-second dropdown label decoupled from the work | NOT DELIVERED |

The three broken links (2, 4, 5) are exactly the "middle" Pip identified. Restated as one
sentence: **research is a fungible, topic-less scalar that staff drip into passively and
undirectably; "ideas" are not objects; and a "paper" is either a bare counter increment or
a player-labelled wrapper around a slug of that anonymous pool.** The felt promise -- direct
a person at an idea, watch it sharpen, publish that idea -- is fiction, not mechanic.

### This gap is already NAMED and DESIGNED in the repo

This is not a newly-discovered hole; it is a **ruled-but-unbuilt** design layer.

- **ADR-0011 (effort economy, ACCEPTED)** specifies the exact missing loop:
  - s3: "Staff effort is per-person, assigned at plan speed to **workstreams from a
    backlog**. Idle staff don't exist; unmanaged staff do -- unassigned researchers
    self-direct per their **agenda**."
  - s4: "**Workstreams** run multi-month and **produce artifacts (papers**, systems,
    campaigns)."
  - s8: researcher model = *lane preference* (agendas: interpretability, evals, theory,
    governance) x *appetites* (incl. prestige/first-author) x quirks; and "**promises made
    to retain staff are ledger entries**."
  - Consequences: "Plan-screen redesign: **assignment UI**, manager housing" -- listed as
    owed.
- **WORKSHOP_2_BACKLOG.md** repeatedly gates this work as not-yet-built:
  - ":468 Research-depth (AFTER L2 gives the **workstream substrate**)".
  - ":469 Capabilities vs safety research are 'ultra-primitive,' need rework + more
    **specificity** + philosophy revisit -> deepen as **workstreams**."
  - ":190 DQ-21 category (4) 'Research direction -- research strategy, workstream steering,
    progress check-ins -> research leads'."
  - ":479 Research-paper events feel old/out-of-philosophy."
- **month_plan.gd:21** in code: staff `actions` + WIP is "the seam **L2 workstreams
  extend**."

So the middle links are the ADR-0011 "workstream substrate" (a.k.a. the L2 build). It is
accepted design, deliberately deferred behind the ADR-0009 month/two-speed layer (which is
partially in: `MonthPlan` / `MonthController` exist; assignment does not).

## 3. Judgment

**Is the gap real? Yes -- ~95% confident, verified in code, not just vibes.** Every middle
link is absent, and the two "paper" code paths are genuinely disconnected. The fiction/UI
over-promises a directed pipeline that the mechanics do not implement.

**How big? Structural, not cosmetic -- but the fix is already scoped, not a new question.**
The gap coincides exactly with the ADR-0011 workstream substrate the team already ruled to
build. So "how big" splits:

- *As a felt-promise fix* (make direction + idea-carrying papers real enough to satisfy the
  fiction): medium, days-scale, well-contained.
- *As the full ADR-0011 L2 build* (workstreams, assignment UI, manager compression,
  agenda-driven drift): large, multi-lane, weeks-scale, coupled to the plan-screen redesign
  that is independently owed.

**Does closing it serve the thesis? Yes, strongly -- this is arguably load-bearing, not
gold-plating.** Three reasons:

1. **It fixes a diagnosed balance degeneracy.** ADR-0011's own context: "one fungible
   currency + state-independent payoffs => constant-policy argmax (`safety_lean`
   dominates)." The current topic-less research scalar *is* that fungible currency. Typed
   ideas/lanes/workstreams are the ruled cure ("with ADR-0010, the constant policy is
   structurally unavailable"). Leaving the gap open leaves the exploit-sweep's core
   complaint unaddressed.
2. **It feeds the promise/liability economy Pip named.** ADR-0011 s8 ties the
   first-author/prestige *appetite* to *promises that are ledger entries* (ADR-0003).
   "Direct researcher X at their pet idea and let them first-author the paper" is precisely
   a promise you make to retain them -- a receivable/payable on the liability ledger. Today
   appetites only gate retention/reveal; there is no action that *feeds* the first-author
   appetite, because there is no directable idea to promise. Closing the gap is what makes
   the appetite-as-promise design actually playable.
3. **It sharpens legible attribution ("every mitigation is a loan").** An idea-carrying
   paper is a named, attributable artifact; a counter increment is not. Attribution is a
   thesis pillar (ADR-0003 "death attributability").

**Interaction with the scoring spine (ADR-0002) -- one hard constraint.** Score is
turns-survived (lexicographic), **flows only, never stocks** (rule 3, "score may never
value anything the player holds at turn end"). So the idea/paper system must NOT reintroduce
a scored paper *stock*. Route its value through world-state flows -- the ADR-0015 doom
streams (`safety_absorption`, `global_alarm`) that `publish_paper` already writes -- not
through a paper count. Papers-as-count already do not score under the current formula; keep
it that way. An idea/workstream is world state that produces flow events, not a trophy.

**Determinism / replay (ADR-0006) -- a real but manageable cost, and a leaderboard fork.**
Any change to research accrual math changes the deterministic sim stream, which
**invalidates existing replays and forks the leaderboard** -- this is a gameplay change, so
it belongs to a version bump, not a patch. Two disciplines to hold: (a) new RNG must use
seeded child-streams (the `researcher.gd:207` `hidden_rng` pattern) so unrelated streams
stay byte-stable; (b) assignment is a *player input* -- record it in the action log and it
replays deterministically for free.

## 4. Plan of attack

All three options are sim changes that fork the leaderboard / invalidate old replays, so
batch whichever ships into a single version bump and note it in patch_notes.

### Option MINIMAL -- "Directed focus + idea-carrying papers" (fast-follow, days)

Smallest change that delivers the *felt* promise without building the full substrate.

- Add a per-researcher `focus_topic` (reuse the `PaperSubmissions.Topic` enum) that the
  player sets via a new lightweight "Direct research" action / dropdown on the staff panel.
  Default = derived from the researcher's `specialization` so nothing breaks.
- In `_step_researcher_productivity`, tag the 1-3 research each researcher generates with
  their `focus_topic`: accumulate into a small `state.research_by_topic` dict instead of
  (or alongside) the single scalar. Route the doom-stream side-effects by focus.
- When a paper is produced (both paths), have it *carry the dominant accrued topic + the
  top-contributing researcher as lead*, instead of a last-second dropdown. The conference
  submit dialog pre-fills topic/lead from accrued work.
- Touches: `researcher.gd` (+field, serialize), `turn_manager.gd` (accrual routing),
  `paper_submissions.gd` / `actions.gd` (carry accrued idea), one small UI verb,
  `game_state.gd` (new dict + save/load).
- Delivers: link 2 (direction, per-person) and link 5 (paper carries the idea). Link 4
  (specificity accrual over multi-month) is only *gestured* at (topic-tagged totals), not a
  real lifecycle.
- Risk: low-medium. No plan-screen redesign. Balance re-tune of the accrual split.
- Explicitly build it as the seam toward MODERATE (topic-tagged research IS the precursor to
  workstream WIP).

### Option MODERATE -- the ADR-0011 workstream substrate v1 (the "L2" build, weeks)

Build the ruled design. A `Workstream` object = { idea/topic, assigned researchers,
accrued_progress, target_artifact }, living on `month_plan.gd`'s existing
`queued_strategic` / `spend_attention` seam.

- Staff `actions` (per-person effort) are *assigned* at plan speed to workstreams from a
  backlog (ADR-0011 s3). Unassigned researchers self-direct per their agenda/lane
  (s3 "unmanaged staff do" exist).
- A workstream accrues progress multi-month (link 3+4 -- real specificity growth); at a
  threshold it *emits a paper carrying that idea*, with quality from accrued progress and
  contributors as authors (link 5, properly).
- Retire the fungible `research` scalar as the primary path (or demote it to a within-
  workstream fuel), collapsing the two paper paths into one.
- Feeds the promise economy: assigning a researcher to their preferred agenda / granting
  first-authorship is a ledger promise (ADR-0011 s8 + ADR-0003).
- Touches: new `workstream.gd`, `month_plan` / `month_controller`, `turn_manager` accrual,
  paper emission, `game_state` save/load, **plan-screen assignment UI** (independently owed
  per ADR-0011 Consequences), rival/sweep-bot policy axes (assignment).
- Delivers: all three broken links, and the diagnosed balance fix (portfolio beats constant
  line).
- Risk: medium-high. Couples to the plan-screen redesign; needs DQ-15 archetype roster
  (Pip owes 3-5 archetypes) for the agenda-drift flavour; bigger re-balance and a definite
  replay fork.

### Option AMBITIOUS -- ideas as a full first-class lifecycle (multi-lane, longer)

MODERATE plus: `Idea` objects with an explicit *specificity dimension* that ratchets
(idea -> hypothesis -> result -> paper) as effort accrues; agenda-driven unmanaged drift
with manager compression / Celine's-law fidelity loss (ADR-0011 s5); promises-to-direct as
first-class ledger entries with departure riders when starved. This is the whole L2 + DQ-15
+ SA-inward + manager layer -- the full fiction. High risk, only sequence it after MODERATE
proves the plumbing.

### Recommendation

**Sequence MINIMAL now as a fast-follow, then MODERATE as the scheduled L2 build. Do not
re-litigate MODERATE -- it is already ruled (ADR-0011 ACCEPTED); it only needs scheduling.**

Rationale: MINIMAL delivers the felt promise Pip is missing within days, de-risks the two
pieces most likely to bite (topic-routing the accrual; making a paper carry an accrued idea
rather than a dropdown label), and is deliberately the *seam* toward the workstream
substrate -- nothing built in MINIMAL is thrown away by MODERATE. MODERATE is where the real
thesis payoff lands (kills the constant-policy exploit, wires the promise/appetite/ledger
loop), but it is gated on the plan-screen redesign and DQ-15 roster, so it should ride the
next L2 milestone rather than a fast-follow. AMBITIOUS is a future exercise, not a near-term
call.

Sequencing:

1. **Now (fast-follow):** MINIMAL. Ship in a version bump; note the replay fork.
2. **Next L2 milestone:** MODERATE, alongside the owed plan-screen assignment UI and DQ-15
   archetypes. Fold the two paper paths into one workstream emission.
3. **Later:** AMBITIOUS specificity lifecycle + manager compression, once MODERATE's
   plumbing is proven by the exploit-sweep.

Leaderboard flag: MINIMAL and MODERATE each change the deterministic sim -> each is a
gameplay change that invalidates prior replays and forks the board. Batch, version-bump,
and announce; do not slip either into a patch release.
