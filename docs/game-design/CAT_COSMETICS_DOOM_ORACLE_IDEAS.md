## 1. The cat as a DOOM-ORACLE (the standout -- ties to doom-legibility / foresight)
- Framing: cats judge doom better than humans. Early game, players have LOW RESOLUTION on the
  actual p(Doom) NUMBER (devs know it; players go by vibes + trends).
- Visually observant players get MORE doom-resolution via subtle game markers. The CAT is a
  higher-resolution doom indicator: if the player's UI shows 3 levels of doom, the cat displays
  4 / 5 / 7 graduations. Observant players read the cat for finer gradations than the raw UI
  gives.
- This IS the "foresight as an earned/observed capability" thread (see the redaction motif +
  foresight-as-purchased idea in COLD_OPEN_SEQUENCE): the cat = a diegetic, always-present doom
  sensor that rewards attention rather than a free HUD readout.
- The cat gets WEIRD at high doom (strange behaviour; maybe teach it to talk via buttons or
  subtly) -- a high-doom flavour mechanic.

## 2. Cat adoption vs the rival stealing it (a REAL decision -- ties to rivals / DQ-22)
- The cat's presence/absence triggers MULTIPLE times per run -- multiple chances to bring the
  cat into the office (not a one-shot).
- If the player does NOT adopt the cat, it may go to a RIVAL doomlab. Whoever holds the cat gets
  mechanistic bonuses.
- Rival ALSO having a cat is cool. Maybe the rival gets the cat BY DEFAULT -> rejecting your cat
  hands the rival 2 STACKING modifiers (they gain + you lose = a swing).
- **TENSION TO RESOLVE (Claude flag):** this makes the cat a MINMAX decision (adopt-or-lose-a-
  modifier), which rubs against Pip's own "don't force minmaxers, leave room for nonoptimal fun"
  principle. Pick a lane: (a) cat is mostly EMOTIONAL/cosmetic with SMALL mechanical stakes
  (skippable without real punishment), or (b) a genuine STRATEGIC rival-swing (real stakes, not
  "optional fun"). Both-at-once undercuts one. REC: keep the cat's mechanical bonus MODEST --
  adopting feels good and is slightly optimal, but skipping isn't a trap -- so the nonoptimal-
  play room survives.

## 3. Cosmetics philosophy [PIP principle]
- Cosmetics can COST money (in-game) but are NEVER forced -- players OPT in for visual
  customisation / easter-egg rewards.
- Recurring principle: nice discoveries + feelings of progress, but do NOT deterministically
  lock players in or oblige minmaxers. There's room for deliberately nonoptimal play in pursuit
  of fun. Minmaxers datamine anyway -- do not sweat forcing them.

## 4. The shoggoth / RSI cutscene easter egg [PIP]
- Recreate a domestic "burned dinner" panic scene (Skinner/Chalmers reference -- CONFIRM the
  exact meme with Pip) with RSI as the punchline: the house server turns into a shoggoth, smiley
  faces everywhere. "Help, help -- the house is recursively improving!" / "No dear, it's just
  gradient descent" / [panic gibberish].
- Mechanically INERT (a little cutscene that does nothing), BUT afterward unlocks a COSMETIC: a
  little set of tentacles + smiley masks that appear on your couch.
- On-brand: dark comedy, AI-safety in-joke, the shoggoth meme (see the earlier "sneak the
  shoggoth in once era-appropriate + permission" note). Ties to the CRT / redaction aesthetic.
